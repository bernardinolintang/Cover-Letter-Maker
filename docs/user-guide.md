## 1. Getting Started

When you open CoverCraft you will see the main page with:

- A **header** with navigation buttons (Profile, Instructions, History, dark mode toggle)
- A **job posting input** area in the centre
- A **generate button** that becomes active once your profile is complete

Before you can generate a letter you need to complete your profile. If your profile is incomplete, an amber warning banner will appear on the main page.

---

## 2. Setting Up Your Profile

Click the **Profile** button in the header to open the Profile Editor.

### Required Fields

These four fields must be filled in before you can generate any letter:

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| **Name** | Your full name as it should appear on the letter |
| **Email** | Your contact email address |
| **Phone** | Your phone number |
| **Location** | Your city or region (e.g. "London, UK") |

### Optional Contact Links

- **LinkedIn URL** — your LinkedIn profile link
- **Website URL** — your portfolio or personal website

### Education

- **Programme** — your degree (e.g. "Bachelor of Computer Science")
- **University** — the institution name
- **Degree Year** — your graduation year (e.g. "2025")

### Skills

Start typing a skill and a suggestion dropdown will appear. Select a skill or press **Enter** to add it. Remove a skill by clicking the **×** on its badge. The app includes 150+ suggestions covering programming languages, frameworks, and soft skills.

### Experiences

Click **+** to add a work experience entry. Each entry has:

- **Title** — your job title
- **Company** — the employer name
- **Start Date / End Date** — leave End Date blank if this is your current role
- **Description** — a summary of your responsibilities
- **Outcomes** — measurable results or achievements (optional but recommended)

### Projects

Click **+** to add a personal or academic project. Each entry has:

- **Name** — the project title
- **Description** — what the project does
- **Technologies** — tools and languages used (comma-separated)
- **Outcomes** — results or impact achieved

### Auto-fill from Resume

At the top of the Profile Editor there is an **Upload Resume** button. Upload a PDF, Word (.docx), or plain text (.txt) file and the app will:

1. Extract the text from your file entirely in your browser (nothing is uploaded until the next step)
2. Send the extracted text to the AI, which reads it and populates all your profile fields automatically

Review the auto-filled fields and correct anything the AI missed before saving.

### Saving

Click **Save** at the bottom of the Profile Editor. Your profile is stored in your browser and will be there the next time you visit.

## 3. Generating a Cover Letter

### Step 1 — Add a Job Posting

You have two options:

**Option A — Paste text**
Copy the full job description and paste it into the large text area on the main page.

**Option B — Import from a link**
Paste the URL of the job listing into the "Import from Job Link" field and click the link icon. The app will fetch the page and extract the job text for you.

> **Note:** Some job sites (e.g. LinkedIn) require you to be logged in. If the import fails, copy and paste the text manually instead.

### Step 2 — Review the Parsed Job Details

After you add a job posting the app automatically reads it and displays:

- **Company name**, **role title**, and **location**
- **Key requirements** extracted from the description
- **Keywords** that will be emphasised in your letter

### Step 3 — (Optional) Research the Company

Click **Research Company** to have the AI read the job page and extract:

- A company summary
- Mission statement
- Company values
- Recent news

This information is added to the Company Context field in Instructions and woven into the closing paragraph of your letter.

### Step 4 — Generate

Click the **Generate Cover Letter** button. You will see rotating status messages while the AI works (typically 10–20 seconds). When finished, your letter appears on the right side of the screen.

## 4. Understanding Quality Badges

After generation, five badges indicate the quality of your letter:

| Badge | What it Checks |
|---|---|
| **No Dashes** | The letter contains no hyphens, en-dashes, or em-dashes — a common business letter rule |
| **No Bullets** | The letter uses only paragraphs, no bullet points or numbered lists |
| **Format OK** | The letter has the correct block structure: header, date, recipient, salutation, body paragraphs, sign-off |
| **Length OK** | The word count is between 280 and 380 words |
| **Availability Mentioned** | Your availability date appears in the opening paragraph |

Green means the check passed. Red means it failed. The badges are informational — you can still export a letter with a failed check if you choose to.

## 5. Editing and Exporting

### Editing

Click the **Edit** button (pencil icon) above the letter to switch to edit mode. You can type directly in the letter to fix any mistakes or personalise the content further. Click **Done** when finished.

### Exporting

Four export options are available:

| Option | Description |
|---|---|
| **PDF** | Formatted PDF ready to attach to an application |
| **DOCX** | Editable Word document (also opens in Google Docs) |
| **TXT** | Plain text file |
| **Copy** | Copies the letter text to your clipboard |

The filename is generated automatically from your name, role, and company (e.g. `Jane Smith Cover Letter for Software Engineer at Acme.pdf`).

## 6. History and Collections

### Viewing History

Click the **History** button in the header to open the history panel. Every letter you generate is saved automatically. Click any letter in the list to load it back into the editor.

### Collections

Collections let you organise your letters by colour-coded groups (e.g. "Tech roles", "Summer internships").

**Create a collection:**
1. Open History
2. Click **+ New Collection**
3. Pick a colour and enter a name
4. Click **+** to save

**Assign a letter to a collection:**
- Hover over a letter in the history list and click the folder icon
- Select the collection from the popover

**Edit or delete a collection:**
- Click the pencil icon to rename or recolour
- Click the trash icon to delete — this unassigns all letters but does not delete them

### Deleting Letters

Hover over a letter and click the trash icon. This permanently removes it from history and cannot be undone.

## 7. Instructions and Settings

Click the **Instructions** button in the header to customise how your letters are generated.

| Setting | Description |
|---|---|
| **Tone** | Choose from Professional, Confident, Concise, Story-driven, or Technical |
| **Availability** | Override your profile's default availability date |
| **Letter Date** | Set a specific date for the letter (defaults to today) |
| **Recipient Name** | Name of the hiring manager (defaults to "Hiring Team") |
| **Recipient Title** | Their job title |
| **Company / Org** | Company name (auto-detected from job posting if blank) |
| **Company Location** | City or region of the company |
| **Company Context** | Paste the company mission, values, or recent news to personalise the closing paragraph |
| **Custom AI Prompt** | View and edit the system prompt that controls the AI's writing rules |

Click **Save Instructions** to apply. Settings persist across sessions.

## 8. Document Library

The Document Library lets you attach supporting files that the AI can reference when writing your letter — for example a transcript, portfolio description, or detailed resume.

**To add a document:**
1. Open the Profile Editor and scroll to the Document Library section
2. Click **Upload Document**
3. Select a PDF, DOCX, or TXT file (max 10 MB)
4. Choose a document type: Resume, Transcript, Portfolio, or Other

The text is extracted in your browser and stored locally. When you generate a letter, select which documents to include and their content will be provided to the AI as additional context.

> **Note:** If the document and your profile conflict, the profile always takes priority.

## 9. Tips and Best Practices

- **Fill in Outcomes** on your experiences and projects. The AI is instructed never to invent metrics, so the more data you give it, the stronger your letter will be.
- **Paste the full job posting**, not just the title. The more detail the AI has about requirements, the better it can match your profile.
- **Use Company Context** for roles you really care about. Mentioning the company's mission or recent news in the closing paragraph stands out to hiring managers.
- **Check Length OK.** Letters between 280–380 words are optimised for readability. If the badge fails, try editing to cut or add a sentence.
- **Your data stays on your device.** The app stores everything in your browser's local storage. No account is needed and nothing is saved to a server (except the AI generation request).
